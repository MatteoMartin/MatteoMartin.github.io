About

I am a bioengineer and a PhD candidate, focusing on the nonlinear dynamics of neuronal and glial cell models. My research explores how complex dynamics emerge in biological systems, bridging neuroscience, dynamical systems, pharmacology, and computational modeling. Recently, I have developed a keen interest in translating mathematical tools for pharmacology to advance drug-discovery and pharmacological-research in general. Beyond the lab, I am an adventurer at heart: snowboarding, hiking, running, tennis, gym, and basketball keep my energy flowing.
